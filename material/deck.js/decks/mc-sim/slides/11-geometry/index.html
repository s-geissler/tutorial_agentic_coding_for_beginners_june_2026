The area ratio of a quarter circle to its square is π / 4

Inscribe a quarter circle of radius 1 inside a unit square. The fraction of the square covered by the circle is exactly π / 4 — so a random point falls inside the circle with that probability.

A point (x, y) is inside the circle when x² + y² ≤ 1.

Square area 1 × 1 = 1
Quarter-circle area ¼ π r² = π / 4
Ratio (π / 4) ÷ 1 = π / 4
