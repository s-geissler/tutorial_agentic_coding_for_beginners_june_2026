Random sampling estimates quantities that resist exact calculation

A Monte Carlo simulation swaps a hard deterministic problem for a probabilistic experiment you can repeat thousands of times — then averages the results.

The problem

Some answers are out of reach

Areas, integrals, and probabilities can lack a closed form or be too high-dimensional to compute directly.

The trick

Build a repeatable random experiment

Design a trial whose outcome relates to the target quantity, then run it many, many times under the same rules.

The payoff

The average converges to the answer

More trials tighten the estimate, and the error is quantifiable — it shrinks predictably as samples accumulate.

Named after the Monte Carlo casino — the method's inventors likened the randomness of neutron diffusion to a game of chance.
