Three things to remember about Monte Carlo

The dartboard estimate of π is a toy, but the principles it illustrates are exactly what scale to research and industry.

1
Randomness solves hard deterministic problems

Swap an exact computation for a repeatable random experiment. The average of the outcomes converges to the quantity you want — no closed form required.

2
Convergence is universal, but slow

The error falls as 1 / √N no matter the problem: halve the uncertainty and you quadruple the work. The trade for that slowness is dimension-independent cost.

3
One recipe, many domains

Define a domain, sample, evaluate, aggregate. From π to neutron transport to option pricing, only the “hit test” changes — the method stays the same.

π ≈ 4 × hits / N  —  ten lines of code, one powerful idea.
