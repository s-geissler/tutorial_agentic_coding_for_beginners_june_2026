Every Monte Carlo method follows the same four steps

Whether you estimate an area, an integral, or a rare-event probability, the recipe is identical — only the domain and the “hit” test change.

1

Define the domain

Specify the region you sample from and the exact rule that decides whether a sample is a hit.

2

Sample randomly

Draw N independent points from a known distribution (usually uniform) over that domain.

3

Evaluate each sample

Apply the hit test to every point and record the outcome — a count, a value, or a yes/no flag.

4

Aggregate the results

Form the estimate from the average or ratio of outcomes. The error falls as 1/√N — quadruple the samples to halve the uncertainty.
